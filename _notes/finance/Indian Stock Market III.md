# How the Indian Stock Market Performed Over 20, 10, and 5 Years

We compare four equity segments with gold and real estate across three time horizons.

**Equity proxies**

- Large cap → **NIFTY 50**
- Mid cap → **NIFTY Midcap 150**
- Small cap → **NIFTY Smallcap 250**
- Micro cap → **NIFTY Microcap 250**

------

## 1. The 20 Years (2006–2026) — Structural Compounding

Over last 2 decades, the companies which could scale the operation grew

| Segment / Asset          | CAGR   | Multiplier | ₹1,00,000 → |
| ------------------------ | ------ | ---------- | ----------- |
| NIFTY 50 (Large)         | ~13.1% | **11.8×**  | ₹11.8 L     |
| NIFTY Midcap 150         | ~15.8% | **18.9×**  | ₹18.9 L     |
| NIFTY Smallcap 250       | ~13.9% | **13.6×**  | ₹13.6 L     |
| NIFTY Microcap 250*      | ~16.2% | **20.0×**  | ₹20.0 L     |
| Gold (24K)               | 14.8%  | **15.0×**  | ₹15.0 L     |
| Real Estate (avg + rent) | ~7%    | **3.87×**  | ₹3.87 L     |

Key Takeaways:

* Mid caps compounded the most among equities. 
* **Gold’s Resilience:** Gold surprisingly outperformed large-cap equities over the 20-year stretch.
* **Micro-Cap Volatility:** While micro caps delivered the highest CAGR, they did so with significantly higher price swings.
* Real estate appreciated steadily with yield.

------

## 2. The 10 Years (2016–2026) — Formalization and Re-rating

In this decade, lot of business sectors moved from unorganized to organized sectors

| Segment / Asset          | CAGR   | Multiplier | ₹1,00,000 → |
| ------------------------ | ------ | ---------- | ----------- |
| NIFTY 50 (Large)         | 14.56% | **3.86×**  | ₹3.86 L     |
| NIFTY Midcap 150         | 22.61% | **7.70×**  | ₹7.70 L     |
| NIFTY Smallcap 250       | 20.54% | **6.50×**  | ₹6.50 L     |
| NIFTY Microcap 250       | 28.55% | **12.10×** | ₹12.10 L    |
| Gold (24K)               | 18.5%  | **5.45×**  | ₹5.45 L     |
| Real Estate (avg + rent) | ~8%    | **2.16×**  | ₹2.16 L     |

**Summary**

* Mid, small, and micro caps sharply outperformed. Capital favored formal, scalable businesses. 

* Gold strengthened. Real estate lagged equities.
* **BSE Market Cap:** The total market cap grew from **₹105 L Cr** in 2016 to **₹465 L Cr** by 2026.
* **Comparison to Assets:** This growth was so aggressive that the equity market reached a scale comparable to India's massive household gold reserves (**₹450 L Cr**).

------

## 3. The 5 Years (2021–2026) — Liquidity Surge then Normalization

The first half of this period was marked by an influx of cheap capital and high global liquidity, which acted as a "rising tide" lifting all boats, regardless of company size.

| Segment / Asset          | CAGR   | Multiplier | ₹1,00,000 → |
| ------------------------ | ------ | ---------- | ----------- |
| NIFTY 50 (Large)         | 16.62% | **2.15×**  | ₹2.15 L     |
| NIFTY Midcap 150         | 24.81% | **3.03×**  | ₹3.03 L     |
| NIFTY Smallcap 250       | 24.18% | **2.95×**  | ₹2.95 L     |
| NIFTY Microcap 250       | 30.24% | **3.72×**  | ₹3.72 L     |
| Gold (24K)               | 23.1%  | **2.83×**  | ₹2.83 L     |
| Real Estate (avg + rent) | ~10%   | **1.61×**  | ₹1.61 L     |

**Summary**

* **The Smallest Stocks Benefited Most:** Liquidity disproportionately boosted the smallest companies, which are often more sensitive to capital inflows.
* **Hyper-Growth in Micro Caps:** The NIFTY Microcap 250 delivered a high **30.24% CAGR** during this 5-year window, the highest across all equity segments.
* **Small Cap Momentum:** NIFTY Smallcap 250 followed closely with a **24.18% CAGR**, reflecting the market's appetite for risk.



Now, as the capital is drying up, market will evolve  from price-driven to return-driven. 

------

## Overall Takeaways

- Over 20 years, wealth came from companies that scaled into mid caps.
- Over 10 years, formalization rewarded mid and small businesses.
- Over 5 years, liquidity lifted the smallest stocks the most before cooling.
- Gold acted as a powerful hedge, especially recently.
- Real estate compounded steadily with yield and low liquidity.

**Implication ahead**

Large caps for stability. Mid caps for durable growth. Select small and micro caps for alpha. Gold for hedge. Real estate for income and diversification.

## Market Size Explosion (2006 → 2026)

| -------------------- | -------- | --------- | ------------- |
| Total BSE Market Cap | ₹32 L Cr | ₹105 L Cr | **₹465 L Cr** |
| Household Gold Value | ₹60 L Cr | ₹110 L Cr | **₹450 L Cr** |
| Real Estate Market   | —        | ₹9 L Cr   | **₹51 L Cr**  |

India’s household gold value now rivals the entire listed equity market. 

------

## What This Tells Us About the Future

We are entering a different phase in 2026.

Past 5 years were liquidity driven. Next 10 years will be earnings driven.

True wealth in India hasn't historically come from the largest giants or the tiniest startups, but from companies that successfully navigated the transition from **Small to Mid to Large.**
