🍣 Morning in Osaka

No rush this morning. If you haven't hit it earlier in the trip, Kuromon Ichiba Market is a 10-minute walk from the Swissôtel — graze a market breakfast of sushi, grilled scallops and fresh fruit from the stalls. Then back to pack up and check out of Swissôtel Nankai Osaka.

🚄 Osaka → Tokyo

The bullet train day. Five moves, none of them hard — and one luggage decision that makes the whole day dramatically easier.

1
Luggage: send it ahead (strongly recommended)
Swissôtel front desk · takkyubin / Yamato Transport
Option A (do this): courier the big suitcases Osaka → Tokyo via takkyubin from the hotel front desk — ~¥2,000–2,500 per bag, arrives next day at Pullman Tokyo Tamachi. You travel hands-free with just daypacks. Option B: take bags on the train — but any bag over 160 cm total dimensions needs a reserved "oversized baggage" seat (free, book it together with your seat on SmartEX).
Best QoL move of the trip~¥2,000–2,500/bag>160 cm = oversized seat
2
Namba → Shin-Osaka
Midosuji subway line, direct
The red Midosuji Line runs direct from Namba to Shin-Osaka in ~15 min — tap in with ICOCA. With kids and bags, a taxi is ~20 min and door to door.
~15 min subwayICOCA
3
Shinkansen Nozomi → Tokyo
Shin-Osaka → Tokyo · ~2h30 · book on SmartEX
Book via the SmartEX app and reserve Seat E — the Mt Fuji side, window. Mt Fuji appears ~40 min after Nagoya, around Shin-Fuji station, on the Seat E side. It's June / rainy season, so cloud is likely — fingers crossed.
~2h30Seat E = Fuji sideJune cloud likely
4
Buy ekiben before boarding
Shin-Osaka station concourse
Grab ekiben (bento boxes) + drinks at Shin-Osaka station before you board — eating an ekiben while the countryside blurs past is part of the Shinkansen experience. Let each kid pick their own box.
Don't skip this
5
Tokyo Station → Tamachi → check in
JR Yamanote / Keihin-Tohoku line · ~10 min
From Tokyo Station, the JR Yamanote or Keihin-Tohoku line reaches Tamachi in ~10 min (ICOCA works in Tokyo too). Pullman Tokyo Tamachi is right at Tamachi Station. At check-in, mention BOTH bookings — Conf QKPSFRBF (18–22 Jun) plus the separate 22–23 Jun booking — so the desk links them and you don't switch rooms.
~10 minTwo bookings — link them

🌆 Evening: Shibuya

Drop the bags, then straight into Tokyo's most famous neighbourhood — crossing, statue, sunset from above, dinner in the buzz.

🚃 ~17:30 · Head out
JR Yamanote: Tamachi → Shibuya
One easy ride, ~15 min on the Yamanote Line. Tap with ICOCA. Exit toward Hachiko Square.
🚦 ~17:50 · The scramble
Shibuya Crossing + Hachiko
Walk the world's busiest crossing, then watch a full cycle from above — the Starbucks/Tsutaya window or the Mag's Park rooftop. Find the Hachiko statue by the station for the photo.
🌇 ~18:45 · The anchor
Shibuya Sky at sunset
229 m open-air rooftop over all of Tokyo. June sunset in Tokyo is ~19:00, so an ~18:45 entry is the sweet spot — daylight, golden hour and the city lighting up. Book the sunset slot online in advance (it sells out days ahead, ~¥2,500ish online).
🍜 ~20:00 · Dinner
Center-gai + Miyashita Park
Wander neon-lit Center-gai, then dinner around Miyashita Park — a rooftop-park complex full of restaurants, from ramen to izakaya-style, easy with teens.
🏨 ~21:30 · Wind down
Yamanote back to Tamachi
~15 min back. Early-ish night — tomorrow is the Warner Bros Studio Tour with a timed entry.

💡 Worth Knowing

📱
SmartEX takes foreign cards
The SmartEX app accepts international credit cards — book the Nozomi a few days ahead to lock in Seat E (and the oversized-baggage seat if you're carrying bags).
🧳
Takkyubin is the move
Sending the suitcases ahead is the best family quality-of-life decision of the trip — subway, Shinkansen, Yamanote and Shibuya, all hands-free.
🎟️
Pre-book Shibuya Sky
The sunset time slot sells out days in advance — book online now, don't gamble on the door.
🎬
Tomorrow: Warner Bros Studio Tour
Timed entry, and it's a ~50–60 min journey from Tamachi — check the entry time tonight and plan the departure backwards from it.
