🌧️ Why Today Is Indoors

The Hong Kong Observatory forecasts heavy squally showers all day, so this day is deliberately built around covered places: a world-class museum, connected malls, a covered tram and canopied night-market stalls. Ngong Ping 360 is skipped — the cable car suspends in storms. Base remains the ibis Hong Kong Central & Sheung Wan, and everyone has an Octopus card, so moving between dry spots is a tap-and-go affair.

🗓️ The Day, Hour by Hour

The structure is simple: one big indoor anchor (M+), covered wandering in the afternoon, and a canopied night market once the worst of the rain usually eases.

🥣 ~09:00 · Breakfast
Relaxed breakfast near the hotel
No early start today. Walk to a cha chaan teng (classic HK diner) or a congee shop near Sheung Wan — congee, pineapple buns, milk tea. Linger until the first shower band passes.
🏛️ ~10:30 · The anchor
M+ — West Kowloon Cultural District
Asia's leading museum of visual culture — huge, modern and fully indoor, easily 2–3 hours. The teens will like the design & film galleries and the giant LED facade. Getting there: MTR to Kowloon Station / Elements mall (covered walkway across) or Austin. Standard galleries are ticketed — buy 3 tickets online ahead to walk straight in.
🍜 ~13:00 · Lunch
Lunch inside Elements mall
Elements is connected to M+ and Kowloon Station, so you stay completely dry. Big spread of options from noodle shops to food court — pick whatever the kids are in the mood for.
🚋 ~15:00 · Covered wandering
MTR back to Central — IFC mall or the "ding ding" tram
Browse IFC mall, or ride the historic double-decker tram — covered, cheap, tap Octopus — from Sheung Wan towards Causeway Bay and back. Grab the upstairs front seats: it's a brilliant rainy-day sightseeing ride through the heart of the island.
🏮 ~18:00 · Night market
Temple Street Night Market + street food dinner
MTR to Jordan or Yau Ma Tei. The stalls have canopies, and you can duck into arcades between showers. Dinner is street food along the market — clay-pot rice, skewers, dai pai dong stalls. If energy allows, hop one stop to Mong Kok for the Ladies' Market.
🧳 ~21:00 · Pack up
MTR back to Sheung Wan — pack for Osaka
Tomorrow is the early flight to Osaka — you leave the hotel at ~05:45. Pack tonight, lay out clothes, charge everything, and get to bed early.

💡 Worth Knowing

☂️
Umbrellas + light rain jackets
Carry both — squalls come in fast bands. Cheap umbrellas are at any 7-Eleven if one gets shredded by the wind.
📱
Use the HKO app's rain radar
The Hong Kong Observatory app shows shower bands in real time — time your dashes between MTR exits, malls and market canopies around the gaps.
🎟️
Book M+ tickets online
Buy the 3 tickets on the M+ website before you go — you skip the ticket queue and walk straight into the galleries, which matters on a wet Sunday when everyone else has the same indoor idea.
😴
Tonight = pack & early night
The Osaka flight means a 05:45 hotel departure tomorrow. Don't let the night market run late — packed bags and an early lights-out beat a groggy travel day.
