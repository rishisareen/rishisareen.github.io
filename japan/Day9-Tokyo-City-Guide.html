🚇 Getting to Asakusa

Base is the Pullman Tokyo Tamachi, right at Tamachi Station on the JR Yamanote loop. Your ICOCA cards work on everything — JR, subway, private lines. It's a Saturday, so Senso-ji gets packed: leave by ~08:00.

1
Tamachi → Mita Station
2-min walk, right next to the hotel
Skip JR this morning — Mita Station (Toei subway) sits directly beside Tamachi Station and has the direct line you want.
~2 min walk
2
Toei Asakusa Line → Asakusa
Direct — no transfers
One train, ~20 min, straight from Mita to Asakusa Station. Tap in and out with ICOCA. Exit toward Kaminarimon — the giant red lantern is a 2-min walk.
Direct train~20 min

🗓️ The Day, Hour by Hour

Temple in the cool quiet morning, Skytree before the weekend rush, then hand the afternoon to the kids in Akihabara.

☀️ ~08:00 · Depart
Leave Tamachi → Asakusa
Toei Asakusa Line direct from Mita Station — ~20 min, no transfers. Early start matters today: Saturday crowds at Senso-ji build fast after 10.
⛩️ ~08:30 · Senso-ji
Tokyo's oldest temple, before the crush
Kaminarimon gate (giant red lantern photo) → Nakamise-dori shopping street (stalls open ~9am — ningyo-yaki, senbei, fresh melonpan at Kagetsudo) → main hall + five-story pagoda. Try omikuji fortunes (¥100 — bad fortune? tie it to the rack and leave it behind). Before 10am beats the worst of the Saturday crowds.
🌉 ~10:30 · Riverside
Sumida River → Skytree photo op
Walk to Azumabashi bridge for the classic shot: Tokyo Skytree rising behind the golden Asahi flame. Then either walk across to Skytree (~20 min) or hop one stop on the Tobu line.
🗼 ~11:30 · Skytree
Tokyo Skytree + lunch at Solamachi
Book timed tickets online ahead — it's a weekend! Tembo Deck at 350m (Combo with the Tembo Galleria at 450m optional). Then Solamachi mall below for lunch — huge food options, plus the Pokémon Center Skytree Town for Ryan!
🕹️ ~14:30 · Electric Town
Train to Akihabara — the kids' afternoon
Direct on the Tsukuba Express from Asakusa, or via Ueno on the Ginza line + JR. Then dive in: multi-floor arcades (GiGO buildings — crane games, rhythm games), gachapon halls with hundreds of capsule machines, Yodobashi Akiba (9 floors of electronics), anime/manga shops (Animate, Radio Kaikan) — maybe a themed café.
🍜 ~18:30 · Dinner & home
Dinner in Akihabara, then back to Tamachi
Eat in Akihabara or stop via Ueno on the way. Then the JR Yamanote / Keihin-Tohoku line runs straight back to Tamachi — ~12 min, no transfers.

💡 Worth Knowing

⛩️
Dodge the Saturday crush
Senso-ji is calmest before 9:30am or after 6pm. Mid-day Saturday is shoulder-to-shoulder on Nakamise-dori — hence the early start.
🎟️
Skytree: book online (or swap days)
Online timed tickets skip a long weekend queue. And a pivot option: if Sunday's teamLab day looks rainy, remember Skytree views need clear-ish weather — check the forecast and swap the days if needed.
🪙
Gachapon = coins
Bring ¥100 coins for the capsule machines — there are change machines in the halls, but a pocketful saves queueing.
🛂
Tax-free shopping
Yodobashi and Animate do tax-free on purchases over ¥5,000 — bring passports along.
