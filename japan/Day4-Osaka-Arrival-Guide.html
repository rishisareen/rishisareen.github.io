🌃 The Evening, Hour by Hour

Dotonbori is a 10-minute walk from the hotel — neon, canal, and Osaka's famous street food. Back by ~21:30: tomorrow is a rope-drop USJ morning.

🚶 ~17:30 · Head out
Walk to Dotonbori
An easy 10-min walk from the hotel. First stop: the Glico Running Man sign — best photo angle is from Ebisubashi bridge — then wander the neon-lit canal.
🐙 ~18:15 · Street food crawl
Takoyaki → kushikatsu → ramen
Takoyaki at Wanaka or Kukuru → kushikatsu at Daruma (remember the famous "no double dipping" rule) → ramen at Ichiran. Graze as you go — small portions everywhere.
🛍️ ~19:45 · Arcade stroll
Shinsaibashi-suji shopping arcade
The long covered shopping arcade running north from Dotonbori — perfect even if it rains. Great for souvenir browsing and people-watching.
🎡 ~20:45 · One last look
Don Quijote ferris wheel building
Swing past the canal-side Don Quijote with its yellow oval ferris wheel bolted to the facade — peak Dotonbori weirdness, and the store itself is a rabbit hole.
🌙 ~21:30 · Back to base
Walk back to the Swissôtel
Big USJ day tomorrow — you want to be at the gates before park open (rope-drop), so lights out at a reasonable hour tonight.

💡 Worth Knowing

📱
Visit Japan Web = faster arrival
Filling in Visit Japan Web before the flight gives you one QR code for immigration + customs — it genuinely saves time in the KIX arrivals queue.
🚆
The Rapi:t is worth it
Book Rapi:t reserved seats at the Nankai counter on arrival — the retro-futuristic blue train is half the fun, and reserved seats mean no scramble with luggage.
🏪
Konbini run before bed
Hit a convenience store near the hotel for breakfast + drinks for USJ — eating on the way to the park saves precious rope-drop minutes tomorrow.
🎢
Set up USJ tonight
Download and set up the USJ app and check your Express Pass details tonight, so tomorrow morning is purely about getting to the gates early.
