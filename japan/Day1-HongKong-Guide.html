🚕 Airport → Hotel

AI314 lands at ~06:15. The clean path with tired kids and luggage: Airport Express to town, then a short taxi. Plan on being at the hotel by ~07:30.

1
Immigration & bags
HKG arrivals hall
Have each person's HK PAR slip + matching passport ready — Indian passports clear at the manual counters, not the e-channel. Then collect checked bags.
Carry PAR slips
2
Buy 3 Octopus cards + grab cash
Airport Express counter / machines, arrivals
Buy 3 adult Octopus cards (both kids pay adult fares) and top up with cash — this is your tap card for trains, ferries, trams & convenience stores all over HK. Pull HK$ cash from an arrivals ATM while you're here.
Cash top-up3 × adult cards
3
Airport Express → Hong Kong Station
Tap in with Octopus
~24 min ride, ~HK$115 pp. First train ~05:54, then every ~10 min. Fast, smooth, no traffic — the easiest way into town.
~24 min~HK$115
4
Hong Kong Station → ibis (taxi)
28 Des Voeux Road West, Sheung Wan
Grab a red urban taxi outside the station — ~HK$35–45, 5–10 min. Easiest option after a red-eye. Free alternatives: the complimentary Airport Express in-town shuttle, or MTR (walk to Central → Island Line one stop to Sheung Wan → ~8 min walk).
5–10 min~HK$40 taxi

🗓️ The Day, Hour by Hour

Deliberately light in the morning — three travellers off an overnight flight will fade by afternoon, so the big evening is the part to be fresh for.

☀️ ~07:30 · Arrive
Bag-drop at ibis — rooms aren't ready yet
Check-in is ~2 pm, so leave bags at the front desk and ask them to message when a room frees up (often early afternoon). Freshen up and head out for food.
🥢 ~08:00 · Breakfast
First HK meal — dim sum
Walkable in Sheung Wan: Lin Heung Kui (classic cart-style, a bit chaotic, very local) or Tim Ho Wan (Michelin, casual, easy with kids). Order the BBQ pork buns.
🚶 ~09:00 · Gentle morning
Sheung Wan on foot — low energy, jet-lag friendly
Man Mo Temple (incense coils on Hollywood Road) → Cat Street antiques → PMQ & the Tai Ping Shan lanes. All within a 15-min walk of the hotel — no transit needed.
😴 ~12:30 · Reset
Back to the hotel — check in & REST
Important after a red-eye: a real nap + shower beats pushing through. Recharge for a couple of hours; the evening is the highlight.
🚡 ~15:30 · The anchor
Peak Tram → Victoria Peak
Head to Central → Peak Tram lower terminus (33 Garden Road) → ride up to the Sky Terrace for the city's signature view. Buy tram + Sky Terrace tickets online to skip the queue.
⛴️ ~18:00 · Harbour
Star Ferry to Tsim Sha Tsui
Down from the Peak → walk to Central Pier 7 → the iconic Star Ferry across to TST (~HK$5, pay with Octopus). One of the great cheap rides in the world.
🌃 ~20:00 · Finale
Symphony of Lights + dinner
Watch the nightly Symphony of Lights (8 pm) from the TST waterfront / Avenue of Stars, then dinner in Tsim Sha Tsui — or ferry back to the island side.

💡 Worth Knowing

🌤️
Why the Peak is today
Friday is the best-weather day of the HK leg — Sunday has heavy squally showers forecast (HKO), so the open-air Peak + harbour evening was moved here from Sunday.
🎟️
Pre-book the Peak
Peak Tram + Sky Terrace lines get long. Booking the combo ticket online in advance saves a real wait, especially with kids.
📵
Have your eSIM live on landing
Activate the eSIM before you fly so maps, ride-hailing and the Octopus app work the moment you step off — no scrambling for airport Wi-Fi.
