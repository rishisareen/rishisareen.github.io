🚆 Getting to Nara

The Swissôtel sits directly above Namba Station, so the day starts with an elevator ride. Take the Kintetsu line — it drops you a 5-minute walk from the deer, while JR Nara is much farther out.

1
Down to Osaka-Namba Station
Kintetsu Namba — below the hotel
Head down from the Swissôtel into the station complex and follow signs for the Kintetsu line towards Nara. Your ICOCA cards work fine here — just tap in.

2
Kintetsu express (kyuko) → Kintetsu-Nara
~40–45 min · no reservation needed
The regular express / kyuko takes ~40–45 min for ~¥680 — tap on with ICOCA and hop aboard. Upgrade option: the Limited Express with reserved seats does it in ~35 min for an extra ~¥520 seat fee — comfy, guaranteed seats together.

Arrive Kintetsu-Nara — the right station
5 min walk to Nara Park
Kintetsu-Nara is the best arrival point — just a 5-minute stroll to the park and the first deer. (JR Nara station is much farther from everything; avoid it.)

🗓️ The Day, Hour by Hour

An out-and-back loop: deer first while it's cool and calm, the Great Buddha mid-morning, lanterns after lunch, and back in Osaka for the evening.

☀️ ~08:30 · Depart
Leave Namba — beat the heat and the crowds
An early start means calmer deer, cooler walking and emptier temples. Grab breakfast at the station or a konbini on the way.
🦌 ~09:30 · Nara Park
Meet the bowing deer
Buy shika senbei (deer crackers, ~¥200) from street vendors — the deer literally bow for food. Etiquette: hold crackers up and out of sight to stop a mobbing, don't tease, and watch little kids' fingers. Over 1,200 free-roaming deer live here.
🛕 ~10:30 · Todai-ji
The Great Buddha Hall (Daibutsuden)
Home to one of the world's largest bronze Buddhas — 15 m tall. Find the pillar with the "Buddha's nostril" hole: squeeze through for good luck (a Ryan-sized kid favourite). Entry ~¥800.
🍡 ~12:30 · Lunch
Naramachi old town + Nakatanidou mochi
Lunch in Naramachi or near the park. Must-do: Nakatanidou near Sanjo-dori — the famous high-speed mochi-pounding shop. Catch a pounding show, then eat the fresh yomogi mochi (¥250) while it's still warm.
🏮 ~14:00 · Kasuga Taisha
The lantern-lined forest shrine
A gentle uphill walk through the woods to the shrine famous for its thousands of stone and bronze lanterns lining the path — deer everywhere along the way.
🍦 ~15:30 · Amble back
Naramachi lanes & Sanjo-dori
Wander back through the old machiya lanes of Naramachi and the Sanjo-dori shopping street — matcha soft-serve stop strongly encouraged.
🌆 ~17:00 · Back to Osaka
Kintetsu back to Namba — evening free
Hit Kuromon Ichiba market if you haven't yet, or Namba Parks / a quiet dinner near the hotel. Then pack for tomorrow's Shinkansen to Tokyo.

💡 Worth Knowing

🌧️
Rain backup plan
If Nara gets rained out, swap to the Osaka Aquarium Kaiyukan — one of the world's best aquariums, complete with whale sharks — or do Osaka Castle + Kuromon Market instead.
⏰
Go early
Morning deer are calmer and the temperatures kinder. By midday in June it's hot, humid and busy — front-load the outdoor stuff.
💴
Carry cash
Senbei vendors, mochi and street snacks are cash-only. Bring coins and small notes for the day.
🥵
June = hot + humid
Water bottles and hats for all three. There's a fair amount of walking, much of it in the open.
🦌
Deer snatch paper!
Keep food, maps and anything paper out of your hands when deer are nearby — they will grab and eat it. Crackers stay hidden until you're ready to feed.
